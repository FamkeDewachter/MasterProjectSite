Bridging the Gap: DAM Features for Cloud Storage

Famke Dewachter
Breda University of applied sciences
Master Game Technology 2025
Thesis Code Tool

Abstract

The entertainment industry, especially game and film production, depends on effective digital asset management (DAM) to organize complex assets like 3D models, textures, and audio files. While platforms such as Google Drive provide easy access, they lack many essential DAM features, including but not limited to version control, metadata tagging, and collaborative feedback. This limitation creates challenges for smaller studios with limited resources. This project addresses this gap by developing a prototype that integrates key DAM functionalities directly into Google Drive, tailored for creative workflows. Guided by a literature review and input from industry professionals, the prototype focuses on version tracking, batch operations, and annotation-based collaboration. Early user testing indicates the tool enhances commenting experiences, although version control remains a complex area. These results highlight the potential to improve cloud storage platforms with specialized DAM features, offering smaller studios a more accessible alternative to enterprise systems and improving asset management and team collaboration.
